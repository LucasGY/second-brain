# RT 🌿 lithos: The same goes for immersion lithography export controls; only the cat is out of the bag for everything. The MATCH Act doesn't help; in...

**@パウロ** · 2026-04-09 16:18

RT 🌿 lithos
The same goes for immersion lithography export controls; only the cat is out of the bag for everything. The MATCH Act doesn't help; in fact, we should be strategically relaxing these (over time) to prevent any commercial alternative from market entry.

Better to control it vs. lose control for good.

Allow the 1970i (in 2026/7) and it enables 14nm. So what. It may enable 5nm at small scale / high costs. Not a big deal, its mainstream now.

But this move raises the bar for SMEE's SSA800 immersion scanner. No one wants to burn yield to help develop it when commercial scanners are readily available.

Repeat every 3-5 years. Full control.
