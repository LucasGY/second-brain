# I added OpenClaw to our team/project several weeks back and it's been interesting to see the progression of utilisation: > Hooked up to GitHub org and...

**@Mateusz Michalik** · 2026-04-22 03:52

I added OpenClaw to our team/project several weeks back and it's been interesting to see the progression of utilisation:

> Hooked up to GitHub org and project
> MCP support on our app so we can assign small tasks/fixes to is
> Added a MD based KB to give it more context about our work
> Gave it access to our design system for an app we're building
> Slowly give it bigger tasks to handle

But I think the biggest change I've seen is once I added it to Slack. Team members are now using it to have a bit of a laugh, banter etc. 

Slack is the best delivery mechanism / interface surface for OpenClaw if you give the bot the arms and legs it needs (codebase, kb, access to the right channels/discussions). Team members are using it to roast each other or get it's take on comments/posts. 

I have a feeling we'll see more of this 'social AI chat' style of working rather than the 1:1 chat we're all used to using privately on our own - makes it a lot more fun, too.
