# Another nice piece from Eric who makes the good point that while short covering and systematic buying have fueled much of the rally, there is a suppor...

**@Neil Sethi** · 2026-04-26 11:30

Another nice piece from Eric who makes the good point that while short covering and systematic buying have fueled much of the rally, there is a supportive fundamental story going on under the hood with earnings.

I've noted in each Week Ahead how earnings expectations have progressed, but Eric had a nice JPM chart in his piece (via @dailychartbook) that shows just how unusual this year has been in terms of earnings expectations.  Annual SPX earnings expectations have risen nearly 4% so far this year while in an average year since the GFC they've typically fallen by -2%.

As he notes "[t]hat is why stocks are back at record highs. The market is not ignoring the war. It is trading on the numbers."

As a side note, if you are wondering if he might be trimming back any of his outsized NVDA and AMZN positions after both hit record highs, he said "I am comfortable with where things stand. The concentration in tech and AI infrastructure continues to be the thesis, and nothing this week changed that view."  His cost basis for NVDA is $21.
