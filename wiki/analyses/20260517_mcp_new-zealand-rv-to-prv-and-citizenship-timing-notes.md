# New Zealand RV → PRV → Citizenship: Key timing and day-count rules

Checked date: 2026-05-17.
Primary official sources consulted: Immigration New Zealand Permanent Resident Visa guidance; Immigration New Zealand guidance on showing commitment to New Zealand for permanent residence; New Zealand Government citizenship presence requirements; New Zealand Government citizenship application guidance.

## 1. Resident Visa to Permanent Resident Visa

Core idea: PRV eligibility is not triggered merely by reaching day 184 in the second year. The applicant normally needs to have held New Zealand resident status for at least 2 years, and then show commitment to New Zealand.

### Key nodes

- RV start point for the 2-year PRV clock:
  - If the Resident Visa was granted while the person was already in New Zealand, the 2-year period usually starts from the RV grant date.
  - If the Resident Visa was granted offshore, the practical clock usually starts from the first arrival in New Zealand as a resident.
- Earliest normal PRV application point: on or after the date when the applicant has held resident status for 2 years.
- Presence requirement commonly used to show commitment: in the 24 months immediately before the PRV application, the applicant must have been in New Zealand as a resident for at least 184 days in each of the two 12-month periods.
- The 184 days do not need to be consecutive, but they must fall into the correct 12-month periods.
- Therefore, if the second-year 184th day arrives before the 2-year resident-status anniversary, the applicant still normally waits until the 2-year anniversary before applying.

### Practical PRV test

On the PRV application date, check all of the following:

1. Has the person held resident status for at least 2 years?
2. Looking backward from the application date, does the first 12-month block contain at least 184 days in New Zealand as a resident?
3. Looking backward from the application date, does the second 12-month block contain at least 184 days in New Zealand as a resident?

If all three are true, the person may normally be able to apply for PRV under the residence commitment route.

## 2. RV/PRV to New Zealand citizenship by grant

Core idea: the citizenship clock usually does not restart at PRV. Time on a Resident Visa can generally count toward the 5-year citizenship presence requirement. A person usually does not need to get PRV first before applying for citizenship, although PRV can still be useful for travel/security of status.

### Key nodes

- Citizenship presence window: the 5 years immediately before the citizenship application date.
- Immigration status during the 5-year window: the person must have had the right to live in New Zealand indefinitely, such as holding a Resident Visa or Permanent Resident Visa. Any RV conditions must be met or removed.
- Physical presence requirement:
  - At least 240 days in New Zealand as a resident in each 12-month period within the 5-year window.
  - At least 1,350 total days in New Zealand as a resident across the full 5-year window.
- PRV is not the starting point for the 5-year citizenship clock. RV/resident time can count.
- Approval and ceremony are separate from eligibility: after applying, processing and the citizenship ceremony can add additional calendar time.

### Practical citizenship test

On the citizenship application date, check all of the following:

1. During the past 5 years, was the person in a qualifying resident status that allowed indefinite residence?
2. In each of the five 12-month blocks, did the person spend at least 240 days in New Zealand as a resident?
3. Across the full 5-year window, did the person spend at least 1,350 days in New Zealand as a resident?
4. Are the other citizenship requirements met, such as character, English, and intent to continue living in New Zealand?

## 3. Example timeline

Assumption: RV was granted while the person was already in New Zealand, so the RV clock starts on the RV grant date.

- 2024-07-01: RV granted / resident-status clock starts.
- 2024-07-01 to 2025-06-30: first PRV 12-month block. Need at least 184 days in New Zealand as a resident.
- 2025-07-01 to 2026-06-30: second PRV 12-month block. Need at least 184 days in New Zealand as a resident.
- 2026-07-01: earliest typical PRV application date, if both prior 12-month blocks each have 184+ resident days.
- 2024-07-01 to 2029-06-30: citizenship 5-year window, assuming continuous qualifying resident status.
- Around 2029-07-01: earliest possible citizenship application timing, if each 12-month block has 240+ resident days and the total is at least 1,350 days.

Important distinction: if the person reaches 184 days in the second year before 2026-07-01, that alone does not normally open PRV eligibility. The 2-year resident-status requirement still matters. For citizenship, getting PRV on 2026-07-01 does not reset the 5-year clock to 2031; the RV period from 2024 can count if the other conditions are met.

## 4. Quick rule of thumb

- PRV: 2 years as resident + 184 days in each of the two immediately preceding 12-month periods.
- Citizenship: 5 years of qualifying resident status + 240 days in each 12-month period + 1,350 days total.
- PRV is useful but usually not required before citizenship.
- Always calculate backward from the actual intended application date, not only from calendar years.

## 5. Caveats

Rules can change, and edge cases matter. Re-check official guidance before applying, especially if the RV was granted offshore, if travel conditions expired, if the visa had special conditions, if long absences occurred, or if the person intends to apply close to the minimum day thresholds.

> ### PRV 实操检查表
>
> 在准备递交 PRV 的那一天，检查三件事：
>
> 1. 是否已经持有/处于 resident 身份至少 2 年？
> 2. 从申请日往前倒推，第一个 12 个月区间里，是否有至少 184 天在新西兰以 resident 身份居住？
> 3. 从申请日再往前倒推，第二个 12 个月区间里，是否有至少 184 天在新西兰以 resident 身份居住？
>
> 三个都满足，通常才比较符合通过“居住时间证明 commitment”申请 PRV 的路径。
>
> ## 2. RV/PRV 到新西兰入籍 citizenship by grant
>
> 核心结论：国籍的 5 年通常不是从 PRV 重新开始算。RV 期间的 resident 时间一般可以计入入籍的 5 年要求。多数情况下，申请国籍不要求必须先拿 PRV；但 PRV 对旅行条件和身份稳定性仍有实际价值。
>
> ### 关键节点
>
> - 入籍 presence window：从国籍申请日往前倒推的 5 年。
> - 这 5 年里的身份要求：申请人需要有可以无限期居住在新西兰的身份，例如 Resident Visa 或 Permanent Resident Visa；如果 RV 有条件，需要已经满足或取消。
> - 实际居住天数要求：
>   - 5 年窗口内，每个 12 个月期间至少在新西兰以 resident 身份待满 240 天。
>   - 5 年合计至少在新西兰以 resident 身份待满 1,350 天。
> - PRV 不是入籍 5 年的起算点；RV/resident 的时间可以算。
> - 资格满足与真正成为公民是两回事：申请后仍有审批时间，获批后还要完成 citizenship ceremony/宣誓流程。
>
> ### 入籍实操检查表
>
> 在准备递交国籍申请的那一天，检查四件事：
>
> 1. 过去 5 年是否一直处于符合要求、可无限期居住的新西兰 resident 身份？
> 2. 过去 5 年的每一个 12 个月区间，是否都有至少 240 天人在新西兰且为 resident 身份？
> 3. 过去 5 年合计是否至少 1,350 天人在新西兰且为 resident 身份？
> 4. 是否满足其他入籍要求，例如品行、英语、继续在新西兰生活的意图等？
>
> ## 3. 示例时间线
>
> 假设：人在新西兰境内拿到 RV，因此 RV 计时从批签日开始。
>
> - 2024-07-01：RV 获批 / resident 身份计时开始。
> - 2024-07-01 到 2025-06-30：PRV 第一个 12 个月区间，需要至少 184 天在新西兰以 resident 身份居住。
> - 2025-07-01 到 2026-06-30：PRV 第二个 12 个月区间，需要至少 184 天在新西兰以 resident 身份居住。
> - 2026-07-01：如果前两个 12 个月区间各自都满足 184+ 天，这通常是最早可以申请 PRV 的时间点。
> - 2024-07-01 到 2029-06-30：入籍的 5 年观察窗口，假设一直保持符合要求的 resident 身份。
> - 约 2029-07-01：如果每个 12 个月区间都满足 240+ 天，并且 5 年总天数至少 1,350 天，可能达到最早申请国籍的时间点。
>
> 关键区别：如果第二年在 2026-07-01 之前已经住满 184 天，这本身通常还不能触发 PRV 申请资格，因为 resident 身份满 2 年仍然是门槛。对于国籍，如果 2026-07-01 拿到 PRV，也不会把 5 年入籍计时重置到 2031 年；2024 年开始的 RV/resident 时间可以计入，只要其他条件满足。
>
> ## 4. 一句话速记
>
> - PRV：resident 满 2 年 + 申请日前过去两个 12 个月区间各 184 天。
> - 国籍：过去 5 年合格 resident 身份 + 每个 12 个月区间 240 天 + 5 年总计 1,350 天。
> - PRV 有用，但多数情况下不是申请国籍的前置条件。
> - 计算时永远从“实际申请日”往前倒推，不要只按自然年粗算。
>
> ## 5. 注意事项
>
> 政策会变，且个案细节很重要。正式申请前应重新核对官方页面；尤其是 RV 境外获批、travel conditions 过期、签证有特殊条件、长期离境、或准备压线申请时，更要精确核算。
