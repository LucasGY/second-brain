# Bank of Japan Balance Sheet Shrinks $98B in Q1 2026

## Summary | 摘要

The Bank of Japan's total assets fell $98 billion in Q1 2026 to $4.14 trillion, the lowest since Q2 2020. Since the Q1 2024 peak, the BoJ has reduced its balance sheet by $590 billion (12.6%). Japanese government bond holdings fell $84 billion in Q1, the largest quarterly decline since QT began.

日本银行总资产在2026年第一季度减少980亿美元至4.14万亿美元，为2020年第二季度以来最低。自2024年第一季度峰值以来，日本银行资产负债表缩减了5900亿美元（12.6%）。日本国债持有量第一季度减少840亿美元，为量化紧缩开始以来最大季度降幅。

## Key Data | 关键数据

### Balance Sheet | 资产负债表
- Q1 2026: $4.14 trillion (lowest since Q2 2020)
- Q1 2026 decline: -$98 billion
- Since Q1 2024 peak: -$590 billion (-12.6%)

### JGB Holdings | 日本国债持有
- Q1 2026 decline: -$84 billion (largest since QT began)
- JGB portfolio: $3.32 trillion (in-line with Q3 2020)
- Since 2023 peak: -$384 billion (-10.4%)

### Policy Shifts | 政策转变
- BoJ began offloading equity ETFs in January
- BoJ began offloading J-REITs in January
- Significant shift after years of aggressive market support

## Implications | 影响
- Adding to bond market pressure
- Reducing market support
- Normalizing monetary policy
