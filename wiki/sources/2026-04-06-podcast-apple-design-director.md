# Apple Design Director: Steve Jobs Feedback Style and Design Philosophy

## Summary | 摘要

Bob Baxley, former Apple Design Director, shares experiences working with Steve Jobs and discusses design philosophy. Key topics include Jobs' instant feedback style, "title-style communication" for executives, and the importance of embracing constraints in design.

前苹果设计总监Bob Baxley分享与乔布斯共事的经历，探讨设计哲学。关键话题包括乔布斯的即时反馈风格、高管汇报的"标题式沟通"，以及设计中对约束的拥抱。

## Key Insights | 关键洞见

### Steve Jobs Feedback Style | 乔布斯反馈风格
- Instant, direct reactions that see to the core
- No elaborate setup or justification
- Clear, actionable feedback
- "Absolutely not, we can't do that in my store"

### Title-Style Communication | 标题式沟通
- Skip the buildup, show the results directly
- Executives already have context, they want outcomes
- Show user's day, end-to-end flow
- "No user sits through all the business logic before using a product"

### Embracing Constraints | 拥抱约束
- Dr. Seuss wrote "Cat in the Hat" with only 220 words
- Constraints clear the mind of unnecessary choices
- Limitations lead to focus and creativity
- Restrictions are paths to freedom, not chains

### "Apple Hat" Philosophy | "苹果帽子"哲学
- When teams get stuck in arguments
- Leaders ask: "What's the Apple way to do this?"
- Pulls discussion from personal ego to company's best interest
- Focus on "Plato's ideal" for users

### Software as Cultural Medium | 软件作为文化媒介
- Designers should know computing history
- From Vannevar Bush to Alan Kay
- Understanding origins of ideas enables embracing the medium
- Personal computing can transform lives

### Three Innovation Stories | 三个创新故事
1. **Sputnik, Doppler Effect, GPS**: Power of environment
2. **Dr. Seuss' Cat in the Hat**: Embracing constraints
3. **Apollo Program**: Courage to persist in beliefs
