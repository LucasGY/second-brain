# Jakub Pachocki on Automated Researchers, Scientific Discovery, and AGI

_Jakub Pachocki 谈自动化研究员、科学发现与 AGI_


## Summary
This transcript captures Jakub Pachocki's description of OpenAI's research
automation roadmap, arguing that model progress should be measured less by
chat quality and more by increasing autonomy across coding, math, and
scientific problem solving.

这份转录记录了 Jakub Pachocki 对 OpenAI 研究自动化路线图的描述。他认为，模
型进步不应主要靠聊天质量来衡量，而应更多看它在编程、数学和科学问题求解上的自治
能力是否持续提高。

## Key Points
- Jakub distinguishes a "research intern" system from a full automated
  researcher mainly by autonomy: how long it can work independently and how
  vague the task description can be while still producing useful results.
- Programming and mathematics are treated as unusually valuable proving grounds
  because progress is measurable, feedback is dense, and capability gains may
  transfer into other domains.
- The discussion suggests that developer advantage shifts away from line-level
  coding and toward problem selection, direction-setting, and high-level taste.
- Hidden chain-of-thought is framed as a safety and interpretability strategy:
  exposing and supervising it too directly may distort the model's true
  reasoning.
- Scientific discovery is presented as a future human-AI collaboration loop
  rather than a pure replacement story, with AI helping design experiments and
  surface new hypotheses.

- Jakub 区分“研究实习生系统”和完整自动化研究员，主要看自治程度：它能独立工作
  多久，以及在任务描述更模糊时是否还能产出有用结果。
- 编程和数学被视为特别重要的试验场，因为这里的进展更容易测量、反馈更密集，而能
  力提升也可能迁移到其他领域。
- 讨论暗示，开发者优势会从逐行编码转向问题选择、方向设定和更高层次的判断力。
- 隐藏的 chain-of-thought 被视为一种安全和可解释性策略：如果过度暴露并直接监督，
  反而可能扭曲模型真实的推理过程。
- 科学发现被描述成人机协作循环，而不是单纯替代故事，AI 会帮助设计实验并提出新假
  设。

## Open Questions
- What evaluation regime can reliably distinguish strong tool use from genuine
  research autonomy?
- How should governance change if very small teams can direct highly capable
  automated research systems?
- Which parts of science remain bottlenecked by physical experimentation,
  institutions, or tacit judgment even if reasoning improves rapidly?

- 什么样的评估体系才能可靠地区分“强工具使用”与“真正的研究自治”？
- 如果很小的团队也能指挥高能力的自动化研究系统，治理结构应如何变化？
- 即使推理能力快速提升，科学中的哪些部分仍会被物理实验、制度安排或默会判断所限
  制？
