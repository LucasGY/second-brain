# Hormones Shape Sexual Orientation: Finger Length, Birth Order & Brain Development

## Summary | 摘要

Dr. Marc Breedlove discusses how prenatal hormones shape brain development and sexual orientation. Key findings include 2D:4D finger ratio as indicator of prenatal testosterone, fraternal birth order effect, and maternal immune hypothesis.

Marc Breedlove博士讨论产前激素如何塑造大脑发育和性取向。关键发现包括2D:4D手指比例作为产前睾酮指标、兄弟出生顺序效应，以及母体免疫假说。

## Key Insights | 关键洞见

### Sexual Orientation as Instinct | 性取向作为本能
- Like native language - we don't remember learning it
- No choice in the matter
- Biology shapes preference before birth
- "Things that happened before you were born affect who you're attracted to 60 years later"

### Finger Length Ratio (2D:4D) | 手指长度比例
- Ratio correlates with prenatal testosterone exposure
- Lesbian women have more masculine finger ratios than straight women
- Evidence for prenatal hormone influence on sexual orientation
- "You can't increase testosterone by cutting off your finger"

### Fraternal Birth Order Effect | 兄弟出生顺序效应
- Each older brother increases gay probability by ~30%
- Boy with no older brothers: ~2% gay
- With one older brother: ~2.6%
- With ~12 older brothers: 50/50 chance
- Mechanism: maternal immune hypothesis

### Maternal Immune Hypothesis | 母体免疫假说
- Mother's immune system produces antibodies against male-specific antigens
- Antibodies accumulate with each male pregnancy
- Affect subsequent male babies' brain development

### Gay Rams Research | 同性恋公羊研究
- Gay rams show biological aversion to females
- Not just attraction to same sex, but active repulsion
- Suggests sexual orientation involves both attraction AND aversion

### Brain Plasticity | 大脑可塑性
- Hypothalamus is "hard-wired" early
- But overall brain plasticity is higher than expected
- Adult synthetic steroid use can significantly alter sexuality and aggression
