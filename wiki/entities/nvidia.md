# Definition
NVIDIA is a leading American technology company specializing in graphics processing units (GPUs), AI accelerators, and accelerated computing platforms. The company has strategically positioned itself at the intersection of gaming, professional visualization, data center, and automotive markets, with its CUDA ecosystem becoming a foundational platform for AI development.
NVIDIA 是一家领先的美国科技公司，专注于图形处理器（GPU）、AI 加速器和加速计算平台。公司战略性地将自己定位在游戏、专业可视化、数据中心和汽车市场的交汇点，其 CUDA 生态系统已成为 AI 开发的基础平台。

## Key Facts

- **Founded:** 1993 by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem
- **Headquarters:** Santa Clara, California
- **CEO:** Jensen Huang
- **Key Products:** GeForce GPUs, NVIDIA RTX, Data Center GPUs (A100, H100, Blackwell), NVIDIA DRIVE, NVIDIA Clara
- **Software Ecosystem:** CUDA, cuDNN, TensorRT, NGC containers, Triton Inference Server
- **Market Position:** Leading GPU vendor for AI training and inference workloads

## 📅 Evolution Timeline

* **[2020s]:** Accelerated Computing Wave — Positioned GPUs as essential for AI, data center growth, and large-scale language model training.
* **[2010s]:** Deep Learning Wave — CUDA ecosystem enabled GPU-accelerated deep learning; became the default platform for ML researchers.
* **[2000s]:** Mobile Cloud Wave — Initially missed mobile but pivoted successfully to GPU computing.
* **[1990s]:** Early days — Focused on gaming GPUs, disrupted 3dfx and other competitors.
